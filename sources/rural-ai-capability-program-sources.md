# Rural AI Capability Program Source Log

Created: 2026-06-07

This source log supports the first draft of the Rural AI Capability Program artifact.

## Starting Sources

### AI Risk And Governance

- NIST, "AI Risk Management Framework"  
  https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework  
  Notes: Starting framework for thinking about AI risk management, governance, mapping, measurement, and management.

- NIST, "Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)"  
  https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/ai/NIST.AI.100-1.pdf  
  Notes: Core reference for risk-management language and public-sector-friendly governance concepts.

### Local Government AI Use

- National Association of Counties, "AI County Compass: A Comprehensive Toolkit for Local Governance and Implementation of Artificial Intelligence"  
  https://www.naco.org/resource/ai-county-compass-comprehensive-toolkit-local-governance-and-implementation-artificial  
  Notes: County-focused toolkit covering governance, procurement, transparency, implementation, and risk questions for local governments.

### Extension And Rural Capacity Models

- USDA National Institute of Food and Agriculture, "What We Do: Extension"  
  https://www.nifa.usda.gov/cooperative-extension-system  
  Notes: Official overview of the Cooperative Extension System and its federal, state, and local structure.

- USDA, "Cooperative Extension Programs Help Strapped Rural Communities"  
  https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/news/blog/cooperative-extension-programs-help-strapped-rural-communities  
  Notes: Useful background on extension as a rural information, education, and community-development capacity system.

- University of Missouri Extension  
  https://extension.missouri.edu/  
  Notes: Missouri-specific extension anchor for community, business, agriculture, and applied education programming.

- MU Extension, "Connecting Entrepreneurial Communities: Bridging Missouri's Rural Economic Divide"  
  https://extension.missouri.edu/news/connecting-entrepreneurial-communities-bridging-missouris-rural-economic-divide  
  Notes: Missouri example of rural community economic development programming and convening.

## Source Rules For This Project

- Prefer official sources, public-sector toolkits, extension/university resources, and government documents.
- Treat vendor materials as examples, not neutral guidance.
- Separate public-sector AI adoption from data center attraction.
- Separate legal advice, procurement advice, cybersecurity advice, and learning hypotheses.
- Capture dates and links for source-sensitive claims.
